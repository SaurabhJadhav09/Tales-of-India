Description

Gwalior Fort, situated on top of a hill, finds its place among the best fortresses of India. It is also considered to be one of the most impenetrable forts in the country. Known for its great architecture and rich past, Gwalior Fort is a must-visit attraction when visiting Central India. Gwalior Fort, a steep sandstone stronghold, dominates the city, with a rich history of battles, imprisonments, and jauhars. It's famous for its two-mile-long, 35-foot-high outer walls, once considered one of India's most impregnable forts. Emperor Babur called it "the pearl amongst the fortresses of Hind." Inside the fort lies Gujari Mahal, a 15th-century marvel built by Raja Mansingh Tomar in honor of his Gujar Queen, Mrignayani.
