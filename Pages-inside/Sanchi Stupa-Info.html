Description

Sanchi Stupa is one of the oldest stone structures in India built during the Mauryan period. The sculptures, monuments, and lush gardens of Sanchi Stupa have been designated as a World Heritage site by UNESCO. The foundation stone of Shanti Stupa was laid by one of the greatest emperors of India, Ashoka, the instigator of the Maurya dynasty. It’s the oldest stone structure in India. Sanchi Stupa has a large number of Brahmi inscriptions. Currently, this Stupa is 36.5 m in diameter and 21.64 m high. The famous Ashoka pillar comprising four lions is also found in Sanchi. These pillars are constructed in a Greco-Buddhist style.
