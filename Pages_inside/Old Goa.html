Description

Old Goa, located in the state of Goa, India, is a place of immense historical and cultural significance. Once the capital of the Portuguese colony of Goa, it is now a UNESCO World Heritage Site, renowned for its well-preserved churches, cathedrals, and convents. The most iconic landmark in Old Goa is the Basilica of Bom Jesus, which houses the mortal remains of St. Francis Xavier, a revered missionary and patron saint of Goa. The basilica is known for its exquisite Baroque architecture and elaborate gilded altars, making it a must-visit for history and architecture enthusiasts. Another prominent church in Old Goa is the Se Cathedral, dedicated to St. Catherine of Alexandria.
