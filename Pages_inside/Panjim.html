Description

Panjim, the charming capital city of Goa, is a delightful blend of Indian and Portuguese cultures, evident in its architecture, cuisine, and way of life. Nestled on the banks of the Mandovi River, Panjim is known for its laid-back atmosphere, picturesque streets, and vibrant cultural scene. One of the highlights of Panjim is its colonial architecture, with buildings dating back to the Portuguese era. The most famous of these is the Our Lady of the Immaculate Conception Church, with its iconic white facade and grand staircase. The city is also home to the Adil Shah Palace, now known as the Old Secretariat, which showcases a blend of Indian and European architectural styles.
