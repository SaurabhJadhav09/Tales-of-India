Description

Bhopal, the capital city of Madhya Pradesh, is a blend of rich history, stunning architecture, and vibrant culture. Known as the "City of Lakes," Bhopal boasts numerous natural and man-made lakes that add to its scenic beauty and provide a tranquil escape for residents and visitors alike. The city's history dates back to the 11th century when it was founded by King Bhoja. Over the centuries, Bhopal has been ruled by various dynasties, including the Mughals and the Marathas, before coming under British rule in the 19th century. This diverse history is reflected in the city's architecture, with landmarks such as the imposing Bhopal Fort and the intricately designed Taj-ul-Masajid, one of the largest mosques in India.
