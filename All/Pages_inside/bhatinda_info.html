Description

Bathinda, also known as Bhatinda, is a city located in the northern Indian state of Punjab. It is one of the oldest cities in Punjab and has a rich history dating back to the ancient Indus Valley Civilization. Bathinda is known for its historical monuments, including the Qila Mubarak fort, which is believed to have been constructed by the Kushanas in the 2nd century. The city is also famous for the thermal power plant, Guru Nanak Dev Thermal Plant, which is a major source of electricity for the region. Bathinda is an important agricultural hub and is known for its production of wheat, cotton, and vegetables.
