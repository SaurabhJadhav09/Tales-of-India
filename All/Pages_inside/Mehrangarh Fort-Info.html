Description

Mehrangarh Fort in Jodhpur is a majestic structure perched 400 feet above the plains, making it one of Rajasthan's largest forts. It houses impressive palaces and a museum containing valuable relics of Indian courtly life, including paintings, costumes, weapons, and items from the Rathore and Mughal dynasties. The fort has seven gates, each built to commemorate victories over the Bikaner and Jaipur armies. The Dedh Kamgra Gate still bears marks of cannonball impacts from the Jaipur army. Near the Loha Gate, you can see handprints of royal satis, wives who carried the Bhagavad Gita while sacrificing themselves on their husband's funeral pyre, ensuring the sacred book survived.
