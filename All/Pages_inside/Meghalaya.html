Description

Madurai, one of the oldest cities in India, is a vibrant metropolis in the state of Tamil Nadu. Known as the "Athens of the East" and the "City of Temples," Madurai is steeped in history and rich cultural heritage. The city's history dates back over 2,500 years, making it one of the oldest continuously inhabited cities in the world. It was the center of the Pandyan dynasty and a major trade hub in ancient times, attracting merchants and travelers from far and wide. Madurai is most famous for its iconic Meenakshi Amman Temple, dedicated to the goddess Meenakshi, with its towering gopurams (gateway towers) adorned with intricate carvings of deities, mythical creatures, and scenes from Hindu mythology.
