Alleppey Venice of the East

  1. Home
  2. Alleppey

Description

Alleppey, known as the 'Venice of the East,' is a beautiful coastal town nestled between the Arabian Sea and a network of lakes, rivers, and lagoons. It boasts a rich biodiversity and is among India's oldest planned towns, famous for its backwater canals, beaches, and paddy fields. Alleppey is renowned for houseboat cruises in its backwaters, a major tourist attraction. It has a rich cultural heritage dating back to the early Sangam Era. Key attractions include Alleppey Beach, Karumadikkuttan, Marari Beach, Kerala Backwaters, Vembanad Lake, Mannarasala Temple, Mullakkal Bhagavathy Temple, and Krishnapuram Palace. It is also considered to be the oldest planned city in this region and the lighthouse built on the coast of the city is the first of its kind along the Laccadive Sea coast.
