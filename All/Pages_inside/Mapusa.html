Description

Mapusa, often referred to as the "Market Town of Goa," is a bustling city located in the northern part of the state. While it may not have the fame of Goa's beaches, Mapusa offers a unique charm and a glimpse into the local Goan way of life. The heart of Mapusa is its bustling market, which takes place every Friday. Here, locals and tourists alike flock to buy fresh produce, spices, textiles, and handicrafts. The market is a vibrant and colorful affair, filled with the sights, sounds, and smells of Goan life. Mapusa is also known for its Baroque-style Our Lady of Miracles Church, which dates back to the 16th century and is a testament to the region's rich history and culture. The church is a popular pilgrimage site and a fine example of Portuguese architecture in Goa.
